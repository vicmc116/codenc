HP Pavilion All in one PC Model 24 -b223w
January 2023
Tear down & memory replacement

All in One had 6GB memory 1-4GB and 1-2GB chip, customer wanted to max out the memory. This model supports up to 16GB So off we go to replace the memory with two 8GB memory modules.

1 - HP 24-b223w back

All in one in for memory upgrade 6gb to 16gb.

2 - Preperation

To begin use a large piece of styrofoam or cardboard. If you do alot of repairs at your desk it is important to do this as tiny laptop screws can escape the eye and cause some serious scratches!

3 - Remove plugs

Lay unit down on flat protected surface and use a tiny screw driver or paper clip to remove the two tiny rubber plugs protecting the screw holes.

3a - Plugs

Try not to drop, when these bounce they go in odd directions and are hard to find, I know ;-)

3b - Exposed screw

After removing the stand, we will be turning these to loosen the case.

4 - Removing stand

Lay flat and lift up on the bottom part of the stand exposing the screw holes.

4a - Torq Screws

.

4b - Torq Bit

.

5 -Screw Driver Torq Bit ;-D

Remove all four screws releasing stand from case.

5a - Screws removed

After screws are removed gently lift up on the stand, it should lift right off.

6 - Stand & Twist

Once stand is removed you can stand up the all in one and using a big screw driver turn the two screws that are on the bottom of the case. this should open the case on the bottom allowing you to continue prying it open along the sides.

7 - Pry open

Use a pry bar or plastic card as your tool, insert it into an opening on the bottom that previously opened when you turned the screws. press the pry bar down sliding it along the case while gently pulling on the case . You should hear clicking as the tabs are released.

7a - Continuation

Continue prying all around the case till it is loose. Do not pull it away as the two pieces will be connected the cd drive stays on the back with a long wire attached.

8 - Case Open

With case open and back resting against the wall, we are ready to begin removing the screws holding the memory cover in place.

9 - Only five screws

Remove the five screws shown in the circles. Removing the stand plate in the middle like i did is not necessary ;-p (the stand plate has 5 black screws).

10 - Screws Removed

With screws removed lift the plate up alittle along each side until it completly lifts off.

11 - Memory plate cover

With memory plate cover removed we are ready to switch out the old memory with the new.

12 - Old inserted memory

Will be removing these old chips and replacing with the new ones. The two metal clasps on the end of each stick hold it in place. gently pull them open and the memory should pop up.

13 - New Memory

Two eight GB chips of DDR4 - PC4 2400t memory.

14 - New Memory 2

Insert the new memory into the bottom slot first it will slide in at an angle then gently press it down till it snaps into the metal clasps on the sides continue with the second chip.

15 - Replacing Covers

The memory cover has two large teeth on the end by the grill they must be inserted first.

16 - Replacing Covers 2

With teeth inserted press cover down and replace the five screws removed earlier.

16a - Replacing Covers 3

Incase the CD got disconnected from the case this is the wiring route it takes 

17 - Closing Case

Place the back piece back on, careful to line it up on the sides then gently squeeze along the sides till you here it click closed. Do this around the entire case make sure its a snug fit!

18- covers & screws replaced 
Turn on the unit, computer registers the new memory chip. Operation succesful!
